What Security+ Is and Why It Matters

CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) is the most common entry credential for cybersecurity roles. A $425 to $700 investment is consistently linked to a $15,000-plus annual salary lift, with more than 60,000 open U.S. postings referencing it. It is also DoD 8140 approved, which unlocks government and defense-contractor positions that are otherwise closed. Our take on whether it still holds up is in is CompTIA Security+ still worth it in 2026.

Salary, ROI, and Who Should Sit It

Roles requiring Security+ carry a median salary near $94,000, and an IT support technician moving into a security analyst role commonly sees a $20,000-plus first-year increase. With the Bureau of Labor Statistics projecting 29% growth in information security analyst jobs through 2034, the return on a few hundred dollars and a focused study block is hard to match.

What Comes After Security+

Security+ is a launchpad, not a destination. From here, CySA+ deepens detection and response skills, while CISSP targets senior and management roles. Pairing the cert with hands-on lab practice is what turns a passing score into a job offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CompTIA Security+ worth it in 2026?

For most entry-level cybersecurity roles, yes. Security+ is tied to a median salary near $94,000, is DoD 8140 approved, and maps to more than 60,000 open U.S. job postings.

What does the SY0-701 exam cover?

Five domains: General Security Concepts (12%), Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations (22%), Security Architecture (18%), Security Operations (28%), and Security Program Management & Oversight (20%).

What score do you need to pass Security+?

You need 750 on a 100 to 900 scale. Community data suggests 85 to 93% of well-prepared candidates pass on their first attempt.
