UBE Bar Exam Prep: The Complete 2026 Guide

MBE, MEE, and MPT strategy, plus the 2026 subject changes you need to know

Sleek Academia • Updated June 2026

Key Takeaways

  • check_circleThe UBE is weighted MBE 50%, MEE 30%, MPT 20%, and is offered the last Wednesday of February and July.
  • check_circleBig 2026 change: the NCBE removed four subjects from the MEE; Family Law and Trusts & Estates may now appear on the MPT through February 2028.
  • check_circleThe MBE covers seven subjects across 200 questions in six hours.
  • check_circleA UBE score transfers across participating jurisdictions, so one exam can open multiple states.

What the UBE Is and How It Is Scored

The Uniform Bar Exam is one portable exam made of three components, administered over two days. Your total score is weighted, which means where you invest your prep hours should follow the weighting, not your comfort zone.

ComponentFormatWeightTime
MBE200 multiple-choice questions50%6 hours
MEE6 essay questions30%3 hours
MPT2 skills tasks (e.g., draft a memo)20%3 hours

The 2026 MEE Changes You Need to Know

This is the most important update for anyone sitting in 2026 and beyond, and most older guides have not been refreshed for it. Effective with the July 2026 administration, the NCBE removed four subjects from the MEE: Conflict of Laws, Family Law, Trusts and Estates, and Secured Transactions. From July 2026 through February 2028, Family Law and Trusts and Estates may instead appear on the MPT rather than the MEE. Practically, that means your essay prep can narrow, but you should be ready to handle family law and trusts in a document-drafting context. Our explainer, the UBE decoded, walks through the full format.

MBE Strategy: Win the 50%

The MBE covers Constitutional Law, Contracts, Criminal Law and Procedure, Civil Procedure, Evidence, Real Property, and Torts. Because it is half your score, it deserves at least half your hours. Drill thousands of questions, but review every miss until you can articulate why the right answer is right and the others are traps.

MEE and MPT Strategy

The MEE rewards issue spotting and a clean IRAC structure under time pressure. The MPT tests lawyering skills, not memorized law: you are handed a file and a library and asked to produce a usable work product. Both are highly coachable because they are about process, and timed practice with feedback moves scores quickly.

Score Transfer and Timeline

Because the UBE produces a portable score, you can sit once and transfer to multiple participating jurisdictions, subject to each state's minimum score and transfer window. Plan backward from the February or July administration and protect the final three weeks for full timed simulations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the UBE scored?

Your total UBE score is weighted: the MBE counts for 50%, the MEE for 30%, and the MPT for 20%.

What changed on the MEE in 2026?

Effective with the July 2026 administration, the NCBE removed Conflict of Laws, Family Law, Trusts & Estates, and Secured Transactions from the MEE. Family Law and Trusts & Estates may instead appear on the MPT through February 2028.

How often is the UBE offered?

Twice a year, on the last Wednesday of February and the last Wednesday of July, with the MEE and MPT on the Tuesday before each.
