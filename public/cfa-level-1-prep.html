CFA Level I Prep: The Complete 2026 Guide

A 300-hour plan, the optimal study order, and how to clear a 40% pass rate

CFA Level I at a Glance

CFA Level I tests a broad curriculum across ten topic areas with heavy weighting toward a few. Passing is less about covering everything equally and more about triaging your hours toward what the exam weights most. Our realistic CFA Level I study plan breaks the schedule down week by week.

The 300-Hour Plan

The CFA Institute recommends roughly 300 hours. Candidates who pass within a tight window rarely try to spread those hours evenly; they concentrate on the Core Four, then spend their final stretch on mock exams and review.

PhaseFocus
Weeks 20 to 12Quant foundation, then begin the Core Four high-weight topics
Weeks 12 to 5Finish remaining topics; begin a first pass of Ethics
Weeks 5 to 3Second Ethics pass for application and scenario judgment
Final 3 weeksFull-length timed mock exams and targeted weak-area review

Why Study Order Beats Raw Hours

An optimized sequence builds foundational skills first, tackles difficult high-weight topics while motivation is high, groups related concepts, and saves Ethics for the end when memorization is freshest. Quantitative Methods comes first because it underpins fixed income, equity, and derivatives math.

Ethics: The Tiebreaker

Ethics carries real weight and often decides borderline results because the CFA Institute can apply an ethics adjustment near the margin. Treat it as two distinct passes: the first builds conceptual understanding, the second sharpens application to realistic scenarios.

Is the CFA Worth It in 2026?

For finance careers in research, asset management, and corporate finance, the charter still carries strong signaling value at a fraction of an MBA's cost and time. The constraint is not value, it is completion: most candidates who fail simply ran out of structured hours, which is exactly what tutoring fixes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the CFA Level I pass rate?

Recent 2025 to 2026 sittings have hovered around 40 to 43%, close to the long-run average of roughly 41%.

How many hours should I study for CFA Level I?

The CFA Institute recommends about 300 hours. A strong plan spreads this across roughly 20 weeks, or about 15 hours per week.

Which CFA Level I topics should I prioritize?

Start with Quantitative Methods because it underpins everything else, concentrate on the high-weight Core Four topics, and save Ethics for the final weeks when memorization sticks best.
